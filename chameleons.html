Panther Chameleon

The panther chameleon (a.k.a Furcifer pardalis) is a species of chameleon found in the eastern and northern parts of Madagascar in a tropical forest biome.
Panther chameleons grow to lengths of around 16-20 inches, with females typically being smaller than males. The specific name "pardalis" refers to its markings, as it is Latin for "leopard" or "spotted like a panther".
